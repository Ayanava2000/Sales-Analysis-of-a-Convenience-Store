# Sales Analysis of a Convenience Store

The goal was to analyze the sales of a set of products in a convenient store located in the US using 1 year of daily sales data from September 2024 to August 2025.

The description of the dataset can be found [here](#The-Dataset).

The summary of the findings can be found [here](#Summary-of-the-findings).

A detailed sales trend analysis can be found [here](#Sales-Trends).

Recommendations based on the analysis can be found [here](#Recommendations).

## The Dataset

The data set consists of the following columns:

ds - Date  
Product - Latte, Donuts, Wings, Sandwiches and Bread  
units - average units of the product sold per store on a given date  
sales - average revenue generated by the product per store on a given date  
price - average price of the product on a given date

## Summary of the findings

The sales of Latte, Donuts and Wings showed more fluctuations than sandwiches and breads over the one year period. Although there is no seasonal trend identified in the data, there is certainly a strong influence of pricing of the products on their sales. From April 2025 onwards donuts and latte had a steady increase in sales. Additionally the price of donuts influenced the sales of Latte can also be identified.

## Sales Trends
**Latte**- When the price increased from $2.32 to $2.47 in January 2025, sales dropped by nearly 15% between January and March. However, sales started recovering from March onward, growing steadily by around 5% per month between March and May, followed by a sharp 13% jump from May to June. June turned out to be the strongest month for Latte, generating the highest revenue of $12,000 at a unit price of $2.475, with around 3,400 units sold.

**Donuts**- As the price dropped from $1.30 to $0.90 between September and November, Donut sales increased steadily from 1,800 to 2,500 units per month. Between November 2024 and April 2025, the price was gradually increased by about 1%, but sales remained fairly stable with only minor fluctuations of 1–2%. A major spike was observed after the price was reduced again from $0.95 to $0.80 in May, leading to a 25% increase in sales that peaked in June. June recorded the highest monthly sales of 3,000 units and the highest monthly revenue of $4,800. However, once the price increased from $0.80 to $1.00 in July, sales fell sharply by 43%, dropping to around 1,700 units sold.

**Wings**- Between September and October 2024, the price of Wings dropped from $3.20 to $2.75, but sales largely stayed consistent between 35 and 45 units per day, with only occasional fluctuations. From November 2024 to February 2025, the price gradually increased from $2.80 to $3.50, yet sales continued to remain stable at around 40 units per day. Even as the price climbed further to nearly $3.80 between March and June 2025, sales showed very little change, suggesting relatively low price sensitivity for this product. After June, prices began to decline slightly, while sales became more volatile with several sharp spikes, including the highest single-day sales peak of over 90 units sold.

**Bread**- Bread sales remained fairly stable when the price was reduced from approximately $3.96 to $3.84 between September and December 2024, averaging around 4 units sold per day. From January to March 2025, a gradual price increase from $3.85 to $3.95 was accompanied by a moderate rise in sales, which peaked at around 5 units per day in March. However, between April and July, sales slowly declined to below 4 units per day even though the price stayed relatively stable between $3.91 and $3.94. A few noticeable spikes in sales appeared in March and August, with the highest daily sales reaching nearly 7 units sold in a day.

**Sandwich**- Sandwich sales showed strong growth between September 2024 and March 2025, increasing from around 15 units to more than 22 units per day, even as the price steadily rose from $5.20 to $5.80. This suggests that customer demand remained strong despite higher prices. Between March and June 2025, prices stayed relatively stable at around $5.85, while sales fluctuated between 18 and 24 units per day with several sharp peaks. From June onward, as the price slightly decreased from $5.80 to $5.70, sales gradually declined and eventually reached nearly 12 units per day by August. The highest daily sales were recorded around March–April 2025, peaking at approximately 25 units sold.

## Recommendations

1. There is a strong positive correlation between Donut and Latte sales. When the Donut price was reduced to $0.80, Latte sales also increased significantly even though the Latte price remained unchanged at $2.47. This suggests that customers frequently purchase Donuts and Lattes together, especially when the combined price falls within the $3.3–$3.4 range. Introducing combo offers could further increase overall sales.

2. The high demand for Donuts and Latte indicates an opportunity to expand the product variety in these categories. Adding more coffee options, or new donut variants could help attract  customers and increase average order value.
   
3. Bread and Sandwich sales have seen a gradual decline since March 2025 despite relatively stable pricing. Based on the recent trends, the expected monthly demand at current prices is approximately 120 units for Bread and 500 units for Sandwiches. This suggests a need to analyze the pricing, product to improve demand for these products.
